### The Structural Wall: Western CV vs. Korean Jaso-seo

Foreigners often make the mistake of thinking a "Resume" and a "Self-Introduction Letter" are interchangeable. They are not. In the Korean hiring ecosystem, the **Resume (Iryeokseo)** is a dry, factual table of your education, certifications, and work dates. The **Self-Introduction Letter (Jaso-seo)** is a narrative essay that explains *who* you are, *how* you grew up, and *why* your personality fits the rigid **Korean corporate culture**.

| Feature | Western CV Style | Korean Jaso-seo Style |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focus** | Individual achievements & KPIs | Growth process, teamwork, and loyalty |
| **Format** | Bullet points, 1-2 pages | Long-form narrative essays (specific prompts) |
| **Tone** | Confident, direct, "I did X" | Humble, reflective, using strict **Honorifics** |
| **Requirement** | Optional Cover Letter | **Mandatory** Self-Introduction Letter |
| **File Type** | PDF or Docx | HWP (Hancom) or platform-specific web forms |

⚠️ **Warning**: Submitting a standard Western-style PDF to a Saramin posting that asks for a "Self-Introduction" is the fastest way to signal that you do not understand Korean business etiquette.

### Why "Manual Translation" is a Recipe for Rejection

Many expats attempt to bypass this hurdle by putting their English resume into Google Translate or ChatGPT and pasting the result into Saramin. This is a fatal mistake for three specific reasons:

#### 1. The Honorifics Trap
Korean is a hierarchical language. A Self-Introduction Letter must be written in a specific level of politeness (*Sipsio-che* or *Haeyo-che*). Machine translators often mix these levels or use "I" (*Na*) instead of the humble "I" (*Jeo*). To a Korean HR manager, this reads as disrespectful or socially oblivious.

#### 2. The "Growth Story" Cultural Requirement
Korean recruiters want to know your "Growth Process" (*Seongjang Gwa-jeong*). In the West, talking about your childhood or your parents in a job application is seen as unprofessional. In Korea, it is a standard prompt. If you don't provide this, you are seen as hiding your character.

#### 3. The E-7 Visa Justification
If you require an **E-7 Visa**, the company must prove to the Ministry of Justice why they are hiring a foreigner over a local. A poorly translated resume makes it impossible for the HR team to justify your visa sponsorship. Your **Self-Introduction Letter (Jaso-seo)** must be written in a way that highlights your "irreplaceable" skills in a language the government officials understand.
